Pillars of Psychology

Biological Perspective
  • Focuses on the biological basis of behavior and mental processes.
  • Examines the role of genetics, neurotransmitters, brain structure, and other physiological factors.
  • It includes : Biopsycology/Neuroscience, Sensation, Consciousness.
Cognitive Perspective
  • Investigates mental processes such as thinking, memory, perception, and problem-solving.
  • Focuses on how individuals process information and make sense of their experiences.
  • It includes : Perception, Thinking, Intelligence, Memory.
Development Perspective
  • Identifies and studies various stages of development, such as infancy, childhood, adolescence, adulthood, and old age.
  • Investigates the influence of family, peers, and societal factors on emotional and social development.
  • It includes : Learning, Lifespan development.
Social & Personality Perspective
  • Explores the process by which individuals learn and internalize societal norms, values, and behaviors.
  • Examines how individuals' thoughts, feelings, and behaviors are influenced by the presence and actions of others.
  • It includes : Social, Personality, Emotion, Motivation.
Mental & Physical Health Perspective
  • Integrates principles from psychology and medicine to understand how behavior, lifestyle, and psychological factors affect health.
  • Encourages an integrative approach to health that considers the whole person, including physical, mental, emotional, and social aspects.
  • It includes : Abnormal, Therapies, Stress, Lifestyle and Health.
Individual Differences perspective
  • Examining the enduring patterns of thoughts, feelings, and behaviors that distinguish one individual from another.
  • Investigating the cognitive abilities that contribute to problem-solving, learning, and adaptation.
  • It includes : Traits, Temperament, Characteristic and Intelligence.
Biological Basis Perspecticve
  • Investigating how the endocrine system regulates various bodily functions and influences mood, stress response, and other aspects of behavior.
  • Studying the structure and function of the nervous system, including the brain and spinal cord.
  • It includes : Behavior, Personality and Emotions.
